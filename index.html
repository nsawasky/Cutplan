Sprint & Conditioning
  • 5 min walk + dynamic leg swings to warm up
  • 6–8 × 40-yard sprints at 90–95% — full rest between reps
  • OR 5 × 200m at hard effort, 90 sec rest
  • Alternate: flat sprints one week, hills or stairs the next

16/8 Fasting — 2 Days / Week

Wednesday — Anchor Day
In-office, skip breakfast, first meal at noon. Works naturally — you're busy, away from your kitchen, and it's a rest day so no fuel needed for a workout.
Mon or Fri — Sprint Day
Whichever day you sprint, that's your second fast. Skip breakfast, run at lunch, eat after. Near-fasted sprinting can amplify fat oxidation. Let the sprint day dictate the fast day.
Lift days (Tue, Thu, Sat) always include breakfast. Never fast on a day you're lifting heavy — you need the fuel to perform and to protect muscle.

Nutrition Notes

  • ~500 cal/day deficit — targets ~1 lb/week without tanking gym performance
  • ~180–200g protein/day to hold onto muscle through the cut
  • Carbs higher on lift days (Tue/Thu/Sat), lower on sprint and rest days
  • IF days create a natural deficit — don't also try to undereat in your eating window
  • Post-workout nutrition matters most after Thursday's conditioning finisher
  • At 197–200, reassess — the last few lbs come off slower, adjust expectations
